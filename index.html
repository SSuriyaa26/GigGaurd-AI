Intelligence Layer
Four AI / ML Engines
Data Pipeline Overview
Raw sources (IMD historical rainfall CSVs, CPCB AQI archives, synthetic gig earnings data) are ingested nightly by a FastAPI background worker into a PostgreSQL time-series table keyed on (date, pin_code, metric_type, metric_value). Every Sunday at 11 PM, a feature engineering job produces per-rider feature vectors: rainfall variance, disruption frequency, AQI severity, zone consistency entropy, earnings coefficient of variation, and temporal flags. XGBoost inference runs Monday 2 AM to reprice all active policies before the weekly premium deduction. The real-time trigger monitor polls every 15 minutes, publishes to Redis on threshold breach, and the FastAPI consumer runs Coherence Score evaluation before routing to clean or provisional payout.
Phase 1 note: XGBoost and LSTM both train on synthetic data (IMD 2019–2024 + NITI Aayog earnings distributions). No real rider data exists yet. Phase 2 replaces synthetic features with actual onboarding survey responses and GPS activity logs. We are not claiming production accuracy in Phase 1 — we are demonstrating the architecture is correct and the pricing logic is sound.
01 / PREMIUM ENGINE
Dynamic Premium — LightGBM Regression
LightGBM is chosen specifically for its ~15ms inference time — the only viable option for real-time premium calculation at policy purchase. XGBoost is used in Phase 3 alongside SHAP for interpretability. Feature set spans five categories: hyper-local weather (7-day IMD forecast, 90-day rainfall variance for rider's pin code, monsoon phase index), environmental (CPCB AQI 7-day forecast, heatwave probability), rider behaviour (weekly active hours, zone consistency score as GPS entropy, earnings coefficient of variation), zone profile (historical disruption frequency per quarter, waterbody proximity index, road elevation score), and temporal (week-of-year cyclicality, festival flag, day-of-week earnings pattern). Target variable: expected_income_loss_pct for the upcoming 7 days. Retrained every Monday 2 AM using the previous week's confirmed trigger events as labelled ground truth.

02 / FRAUD DETECTION
Multi-Layer Fraud Stack — Coherence Score + Isolation Forest
Three layers. Layer A (Isolation Forest): detects anomalous claim contexts using time since last GPS ping, distance from dark store at trigger time, claimed active hours vs. GPS movement entropy, and claim-to-premium ratio vs. historical baseline. Anomaly score >0.72 triggers escalation. Layer B (GPS Spoofing Prevention): cross-references GPS coordinates with accelerometer + gyroscope sensor fusion. Mock location apps produce zero gyroscope variance — our SDK detects this immediately. Critically, GPS-spoofing apps inject clean, high-precision coordinates; a genuine rider in heavy rain receives degraded GPS with elevated HDOP values. This inversion — clean GPS during severe weather — is one of our strongest single spoofing indicators. The Coherence Score aggregates all signal dimensions (accelerometer variance, gyroscope, battery drain, network signal strength, app foreground state, zone familiarity) into a 0–1 score. Score >0.65: clean payout. Score 0.35–0.65: Provisional Payout. Score <0.35: full freeze pending human review. Layer C (Idempotency): each payout hashed against a unique (rider_id, trigger_event_id, pin_code, timestamp) tuple in PostgreSQL. One payout per rider per policy period, guaranteed.

03 / FORECASTING
7-Day Disruption Forecasting — LSTM (Unidirectional + Temporal Attention)
Architecture: unidirectional 2-layer LSTM with a temporal attention mechanism over the final 7 timesteps (PyTorch). Bidirectionality is explicitly excluded: a bidirectional LSTM reads future timesteps during the backward pass at training time, which constitutes data leakage for a causal forward-forecasting task. Unidirectional LSTM processes the sequence left-to-right only, preserving causal integrity. The temporal attention layer visualises which past disruption days most influenced the current 7-day risk window — useful both for model debugging and for the SHAP-style rider explanations. Input: 90-day rolling window of (daily_rainfall, aqi_max, disruption_flag, order_count_index) per pin-code cluster. Output: P(disruption_day) for each of the next 7 days per pin-code cluster. This forecast directly modulates the Season_Index in the premium formula and pre-stages liquidity reserves before events occur. Training: IMD + CPCB time series from data.gov.in augmented with synthetic disruption sequences.

Adversarial Defense & Anti-Spoofing Strategy
Confirmed Threat: Coordinated GPS-Spoofing Syndicate

A sophisticated syndicate of 500 delivery workers in a tier-1 city successfully exploited a competing beta parametric platform. Organising via Telegram, they used advanced GPS-spoofing apps to fake their locations inside red-alert weather zones while sitting safely at home, triggering mass false payouts and draining the liquidity pool. Simple GPS coordinate verification is officially obsolete. GigGuard's response is documented in full below.

Core Insight: A GPS coordinate is a claim, not evidence. It tells us where a phone's software believes it is located. It does not tell us whether the phone is in a rider's hand in a flooded street or on a table running a spoofing app. The difference is detectable — but only by requiring the GPS signal to be coherent with the physical and behavioural context that genuine field presence produces. GigGuard does not attempt to catch GPS spoofing by verifying GPS. It catches it by checking that GPS is consistent with everything else.
1. The Differentiation: Coherence Score

For every claim event, GigGuard computes a Coherence Score (0–1) across the signal dimensions below. No single dimension flags a claim in isolation. Only a weighted aggregate of multiple low-coherence signals triggers escalation. Score >0.65: clean payout. Score 0.35–0.65: Provisional Payout (50% instant). Score <0.35: full freeze.

2. The Data: Detecting a Coordinated Fraud Ring

Individual spoofing is hard to scale invisibly. A coordinated ring of 50–500 riders produces statistical anomalies at the zone level that are detectable independently of any individual rider's Coherence Score.

Claim Surge Velocity
Genuine disruptions produce a staggered claim curve: riders gradually realise they cannot work and trigger over a 40–90 minute window after threshold breach. Coordinated fraud produces a sharp, near-simultaneous spike because the coordinator sent a single "claim now" Telegram message. GigGuard monitors claim arrival rate per pin-code cluster. If >15% of active policyholders in a single pin code file claims within a 20-minute window, the entire batch enters elevated scrutiny regardless of individual coherence scores.
Cross-Rider Environmental Coherence Mismatch
In a genuine zone-wide disruption, all riders in the affected area show degraded network signal, poor GPS fix quality, and similar accelerometer inactivity as they shelter. If a cluster of 30 riders all show clean network, high GPS quality, and zero accelerometer variance while simultaneously claiming weather entrapment, the population-level signal is inconsistent with what the environment should produce. This cross-rider check operates on the zone aggregate, not individual riders.
Dark Store Order-Count Cross-Check
If 40 riders in a zone claim weather-induced zero income but the dark store's mock order-count API shows only a 15% drop in dispatch volume, the claim population is inconsistent with platform-side ground truth. No genuine mass disruption leaves the dark store nearly operational while every rider claims they cannot work. A confirmed divergence triggers an immediate ring-level investigation and withholds all pending payouts from that zone until reviewed.
6-Hour Policy Seasoning Window
Genuine riders purchase coverage week-to-week. A coordinated ring needs to sign up before the event and file claims during it. GigGuard applies a 6-hour seasoning window: any policy activated within 6 hours of a published IMD red alert for the rider's pin code is ineligible for payout under that same event. Multi-week policyholders are completely unaffected. This closes the most straightforward ring entry vector without touching a single genuine user.
Device and Registration Clustering
During onboarding, GigGuard passively records device fingerprint components (OS version, screen resolution, install timestamp). A cluster of new registrations sharing similar fingerprint characteristics, registering within 48 hours of each other, and concentrating in the same pin code matches known syndicate onboarding behaviour. These accounts are flagged for elevated monitoring before any claim is made.
Claim Velocity Before a Known Weather Event
A surge in new policy signups in a specific pin code within 6 hours of a published IMD red alert is a strong syndicate precursor signal — the coordinator informed the group and they rushed to buy coverage. This signal is monitored continuously and feeds directly into the seasoning window decision logic above.
3. The UX Balance: Protecting Honest Riders

The worst outcome for GigGuard is not a successful fraud. It is Karthik, phone battery at 11%, GPS degraded by rain, sitting under a petrol bunk in a flooded street, having his payout frozen because our system read his genuine distress as suspicious. The defense is calibrated with a deliberate asymmetry: we are designed to be slow to freeze and fast to release. We accept a higher fraud loss rate at the margins before we accept false positives on legitimate claims.

Provisional Payout Workflow (Coherence Score 0.35–0.65)
1
Immediate Partial Disbursement
50% of the calculated payout is credited to the rider's UPI wallet within 90 seconds of the flag being raised. A genuine rider in distress is never left with zero. The remaining 50% is held in escrow pending passive resolution.
2
Passive 4-Hour Observation Window
The system continues collecting sensor and network data silently for 4 hours. No action is required from the rider. In most genuine cases, coherence signals self-correct naturally as the rider moves, network stabilises, and app activity resumes.
3
Auto-Release on Signal Recovery
If Coherence Score recovers above 0.65 within the 4-hour window, the held 50% is released automatically. The rider receives one push notification: "Your full payout has been confirmed and credited." No form. No appeal. Nothing to do.
4
Optional Soft Verification Prompt (Edge Cases Only)
If signals remain ambiguous after 4 hours, the app sends one optional prompt: "A quick 10-second video of your surroundings can help us release your remaining payout faster." This is entirely optional. Declining routes to human review instead. There is no penalty for declining.
5
Human Review with 24-Hour SLA
A claims analyst reviews the full signal record. Account standing, zone familiarity, earnings history, and prior claim record all weight toward approval. First-time flagged riders with clean prior history receive a strong default toward approval. The benefit of the doubt is structural, not discretionary.
What GigGuard Never Does
  • Auto-rejects a claim based solely on a low Coherence Score
  • Requires a rider to prove they were present before receiving any payout
  • Penalises future premiums for a single flagged claim that was ultimately approved
  • Withholds 100% of a payout while an investigation is open for scores above 0.35
  • Treats network degradation in bad weather as a spoofing signal (it is the opposite)
  • Uses a single sensor reading as the sole basis for any decision
